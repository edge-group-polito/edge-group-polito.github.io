Prof. Maurizio Martina, professor of electronics at Politecnico di Torino, reports to
SIE — the Italian Electronics Society — that, thanks to the PNRR SERICS project and with
the technical support of Fondazione Chips-IT, a chip in TSMC 65 nm technology has been
sent to fabrication.

The chip, named Polheepo, is based on a RISC-V processor together with several
accelerators for artificial intelligence and post-quantum cryptography.
