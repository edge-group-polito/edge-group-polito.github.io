It's called Polheepo — flexible, customizable, and built for businesses: the new chip
developed by DET, Politecnico di Torino, as part of the Fondazione SERICS project harnesses
the potential of the open RISC-V architecture to adapt to specific production needs,
overcoming the limits of traditional devices, which are often closed and hard to modify.

🤖 Currently in the testing phase, Polheepo integrates accelerators for artificial
intelligence and post-quantum cryptography, making it suitable for edge, embedded, and
low-power applications across multiple sectors. After the initial baseline tests,
experiments are now underway on engineered boards, with possible applications in the
biomedical and agricultural fields.
